CSS Topic - Gradients

Linear Gradients

A linear gradient is a smooth transition between two colors. To create it, you must define two color stops. A gradient also includes a direction. The colors could transition from the top to the bottom, or from left to right. Here is an example of a diagonal linear gradient- the first color stop begins in the lower left corner, transitions to the second color stop in the middle, then transitions to the third color stop on the top right corner.

Defining Angles

You can also define the angle of the gradient. To top is “0deg”, and to bottom is “180deg”. To right is “90deg”. Diagonally is “45deg”

Radial Gradients

Hello.

A radial gradient is defined by its center. Think of the sun- it starts in the center and moves out. You must define at least two color stops to create a radial gradient. The default will be evenly spaced color stops. You can also create unevenly spaced color stops.
